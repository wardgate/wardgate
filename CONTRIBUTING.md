Thanks for your interest in contributing!

This is sometimes called "inbound = outbound" - the license for contributions in is the same as the license for the project out.

You retain copyright to your contributions. You do not need to sign a CLA.

### No third-party copying without disclosure

Do not contribute code or other materials copied from third-party sources unless:

- the source and license are clearly identified, and
- the license is compatible with this project’s license and distribution model, and
- required notices are preserved.

### Contributions on behalf of an employer

If your employer or another entity may own rights in your contribution, ensure you have authorization to contribute.

For substantial employer-owned contributions, we may request a corporate authorization (e.g., a Corporate CLA or equivalent) before merging.

## How to Contribute

1. Fork the repository and create a feature branch.
2. Write tests for your changes, then implement.
3. Run `go test ./...` and `go fmt ./...` before submitting.
4. Open a pull request with a clear description of what and why.

## Reporting Issues

Open a GitHub issue. Include steps to reproduce, expected behavior, and actual behavior.

## Code of Conduct

Be respectful. Harassment or abusive behavior will not be tolerated.
